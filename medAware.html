MedAware:

medAware is a mobile app that allows a person to look up medication to find what foods to monitor or avoid while on specific medications. Along with side effects associated with the medication.

While my mother was caring for my grandmother after she fell ill, she expressed her frustration at dietary restriction not always being easy to find, and the effects this may have on the body or medication. Through this, I developed an app that easily allows one to look up specific medications and has a full list of foods that should be avoided or monitored while on the medications.

Problem Statement:

Access to comprehensive prescription information is often challenging and can be frustrating for individuals, leaving them with unanswered questions. This problem is even greater caregivers, especially when family members step in to be caregivers. Finding reliable resources and relaying this information can be difficult and challenging. The lack of easily accessible and user-friendly resources for medication-related dietary restriction and side effects can lead to unwanted dietary choices that negatively interact with medication, potentially causing further health risks and reduced medication effectiveness.

Solution:

I collaborated with Clemencia Rodriguez, a 5th-year pharmacy student, to make complex medical information more accessible. Together, we translated medical terms into user-friendly language. With Clemencia's guidance, I developed medAware, a mobile app, to empower users with easy access to medication-food interaction information. This app enhances medication management, user confidence, and overall health outcomes for prescription drug users.

UX/UI Process:

Research:
During my initial research, I interviewed multiple individuals who are care givers to family members, or work in the at home nursing industry. Many of these interviewees stressed, that the interface of the app needs to be large enough for them to read, easily understandable, and ‘not fussy’ to use.

Interface Design:
The color scheme of the app followed the colors of pharmacies, and many medical professions. Teal, blue, orange white, and black are the primaries. Secondary Colors consist of green and red.

For the typography, Navigo was chosen. Because this typeface is thicker, it is easier for users to read. Black was used for the type face to encourage enough contrast for users to easily read the medical information they are looking up.

Buttons are large so users recognize them and help with touch. Button shape is maintained is the same throughout the entire app to help with the consistency of user interactions and navigation.

Lo-fi Wireframes
In the lo-fi stage of wireframing, I kept in mind the age range for this app is wide, and technological gaps needed to be bridged for all users to have an intuitive and enjoyable user experience. Clearly organized information was crucial for the success of the app’s interface and usability.

Hi-fi Wireframes:
In the hi-fi wireframing stage, we prioritized micro-interactions like voice-to-text input, task-saving, and smooth transitions between app screens. These micro-interactions significantly improved the user experience, adding convenience, intuitiveness, and immediate feedback. User testing confirmed their positive impact on creating a responsive and user-centric app.

What's Next for MedAware?

In the future, we would like to expand this app so people can compile a list of currently prescribed medications and review a full list of foods to avoid. The ability to link this app to local pharmacies for alerts when medations are ready for pick up.

Result:

Collaborating with Clemencia, we harnessed this valuable information to develop an intuitive mobile application. This app serves as a comprehensive resource, offering individuals a deeper understanding of which foods to carefully consider or keep an eye on. Whether individuals are proactively managing their dietary choices or assisting others in doing so, our app provides valuable insights. It's important to note that we recommend all users consult with their healthcare provider before initiating any new medication or dietary regimen, ensuring their health and well-being are prioritized.
