kpopstop

Our website was founded by a group of Interactive Digital Media Masters students at Trinity College Dublin with a passion for k-pop and a desire to provide a platform for k-pop enthusiasts and visitors to Seoul to connect and discover the rich history of k-pop entertainment buildings in the city.


Brief:

Design a website that focuses on tourism for a specific building or buildings to a specific audience. Directive is to use HTML and CSS to create a functioning and responsive site that is also compatible with mobile phones.



Problem:

K-pop has rapidly evolved into a worldwide phenomenon. Numerous K-pop enthusiasts aspire to travel to Korea to delve into its rich history and innovative architectural buildings associated with K-pop. However, a significant portion of the available tour sites cater primarily to native Korean speakers. For non-Korean speakers, the process of locating reputable tour sites that offer comprehensive access to tours for each building can often be confusing and exasperating.

Research:

Thorough research was essential for comprehending the distinctive logos, aesthetics, and color palettes of each company and building. This knowledge was pivotal in understanding the alignment of K-pop groups with their respective companies. Utilizing this information, we systematically organized colors, themes, and aesthetics.

To gain insights into these elements, we extensively examined popular K-pop blogs, K-pop group Instagram pages, TikToks, Twitter accounts, and fan pages across various social media platforms. The spectrum of K-pop colors and aesthetics spans from vibrant and dynamic to serene and monochromatic. These aesthetic tendencies also exhibit variations within different groups under each company.

Defining the Scope:

Our team thoroughly examined the most successful K-pop company buildings, meticulously compiling a comprehensive list of K-pop groups affiliated with each of these establishments. Drawing from the insights gathered during our interviews, we strategically refined the scope of our information and content.

This process allowed us to hone in on specific areas of focus and further define our target market with precision.

Target Market

Primary Users:
K-pop fans who are looking for a unique fan experience that allows them to research the history and evolution of K-pop company buildings.

Secondary Users:
Tourists visiting Seoul looking for a fun and exciting time, who may not be fans but familiar with K-pop and curious about learning more.

UX/UI Design

User-Centric Design:
To ensure our platform resonated deeply with users, we adopted an iterative design process that put users at the forefront. The insights gathered from interviews guided us in refining our focus. With precision, we identified key areas of information and content that would captivate our audience. This process not only honed our direction but also refined our understanding of the specific segments within our target market.

Our aim was to create an immersive experience that bridges K-pop enthusiasts with the world they admire. Through thoughtful user experience design, we crafted intuitive navigation and interactions that effortlessly guide users through the vibrant world of K-pop company buildings. Our platform's architecture was carefully curated, allowing users to explore affiliated K-pop groups seamlessly.

Wireframes:
In the early stage of our designs, we used hand-drawn low-fidelity (low-fi) emphasizing usability and clarity. We sketched out the basic arrangement of navigation menus, content sections, and interactive elements. Typography, though basic, played a crucial role in establishing hierarchy and readability.

After gaining an idea of key elements and page layout we moved to digital low-fi wireframes. These digital sketches were the blueprint for our user interface, representing the foundational structure of our platform. During this phase, we focused on the core elements and layout, prioritizing functionality and user flow over aesthetics.

As our design vision evolved, we transitioned to high-fidelity (high-fi) wireframes. These wireframes were more refined, incorporating the visual aesthetics and stylistic choices that we drew from the rich and diverse world of K-pop. In the high-fi wireframes, every design element received meticulous attention. Typography choices were refined to align with the specific aesthetics of different sections. Color palettes were curated to convey the essence of both K-pop as a whole and the unique attributes of each company building.

Interface Design

The user interface became our canvas for transforming complex connections into captivating visuals. Borrowing inspiration from K-pop's diverse aesthetic spectrum, we connect bold colors and poppy elements on a black backdrop. Each aesthetic type found its place, mirroring the diversity within K-pop itself. Every design element, from typography to color palette, was curated to convey the essence of both K-pop and the unique company buildings.



Responsiveness Design:
Acknowledging the varied digital landscape, our responsive design creates accessibility for the variety of devices our users will access the site. Whether our users engaged with us through K-pop blogs, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter, or fan pages, our platform was a seamless extension. The UI's adaptability ensured a harmonious experience across devices and platforms, solidifying our connection with the K-pop community.



Results:
Through a meticulous journey that began with an intense ideation session, to sketching the foundations of our user interface, prioritizing functionality and user flow. As we progressed to high-fi wireframes, we breathed life into our design, drawing inspiration from K-pop's vibrant aesthetic spectrum. Bold colors and poppy elements danced on a black canvas, mirroring the diversity inherent in K-pop itself. Each design element, from typography to color palette, was thoughtfully curated to convey the essence of both K-pop and the unique company buildings. The result is a captivating visual journey that seamlessly guides users through the world of K-pop, celebrating its dynamic energy and creative spirit while ensuring a user-friendly and immersive experience for enthusiasts worldwide.
