Aimitis
Case Study

Thesis Project for Interactive Digital Media M.Sc.
Roles: Project Manager
Lead UX/UI Designer
Content Researcher

Introduction:

Disguised as a skincare app, Aimitis is focused on safely empowering women to regain control of their life by seeking help from toxic relationships and begin the process of healing. Our focus is clear: education, intervention, and protection.

Aimitis is a progressive web app (PWA). Why did we decide to create a PWA instead of a native app? In our research of domestic violence prevention apps in Ireland, we became aware of a significant barrier. Anyone who purchased an iPhone in a foreign country could not download Ireland-specific apps. Many of the women in our group where from China or America we were unable to download and use these services.

Problem Statement:

Alarming statistics highlight the persistent issue of domestic violence, with 47% of European women aged 19-29 experiencing psychological violence since age 15. In Ireland, 1 in 6 Irish women faces physical or sexual violence from their partner by age 15. Shockingly, 49% of abused women globally never report or seek help, suggesting even higher unacknowledged numbers.

In our comprehensive research we discovered three critical voids in the education and protection against domestic violence in Ireland:

1. Accessing Support: Existing online support systems suffer from broken links an inadequate coverage for both the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland. The interface is outdated and cumbersome, especially for users with non-EU devices, creating barriers for women seeking help.
2. Youth Education Gap: Current resources fail to engage young women entering the dating world. Traditional educational methods do not align with today’s youth, who prefer short video formats like reels. This lack of relevant, up-to-date information perpetuates an educational deficit.
3. Obtaining a cord order of protection relies on women’s ability to provide evidence of abuse, posing a significant risk for those living with their abuser. Safely compiling crucial information can become high risk, potentially escalating the dangers they face.

Our Goal:

Aimitis aims to provide a secure and user-friendly platform for young women (aged 16-26, but open to all women) to access education, prevention, and protection resources. By using a skincare app disguise, we protect users from potential abuser’s violating their privacy. A secure PIN safeguards the true nature of the platform, ensuring user privacy.


we are committed to educating young women about abuse types, warning signs, and healthy relationships through comprehensive articles and disguised skincare-themed reels, and quizzes.



Our platform offers a complete directory of women’s refugee centers in Ireland, providing easy access to contact information and essential resources for women in need.



Aimitis offers a secure journaling feature accessible via user-generated PIN, providing a confidential space for women to document their experiences, including uploading images, especially when pursuing legal action.

User Research:

Our team extensively researched domestic abuse globally and in Ireland, including analyzing articles, survivor testimonies, and legal resources. We categorized our findings into sections like abuse types, warning signs, healthy relationships, and readiness, forming the basis for articles and skincare-themed reels.

We consulted professionals for insights on discreetly seeking help. Locating women’s refugee centers in Ireland was challenging due to closures, relocations, and outdated information, requiring cross-referencing with organizations like Safe Ireland, Women’s Aid, and Google for updates in both the Republic and Northern Ireland.

Visual Design:

In shaping Aimitis’ visual identity, we aimed to support women in Ireland while promoting domestic violence prevention. The name ‘Aimitis,’ inspired by the Irish word for amethyst, symbolizes healing and the color purple, globally recognized for domestic violence awareness, aligns with our core values and mission.

To infuse a depth of sophistication into our apps design, we used four distinct primary purple colors, including a rich, deep purple for the text, replacing the conventional black. The choice adheres to our visual theme but also ensure a more harmonious user experience. For the background color, we opted for an off-white shade, providing a gentler contrast compared to a stark white, which can be jarring for users.

Our logo, a striking presentation of an Aimitis crystal adorned with three smaller crystals, embodies the three core pillars of our app.

Wireframing & Prototyping:

When first sketching out our wireframes, we know information needed to be categorized in a logical way, that is both engaging and intuitive for the users. Our wireframes prioritized clarity and logical organization of content, clean, minimalistic interface with engaging visuals, particularly in articles.

Initially, we disguised every section as skincare-related, but user testing revealed confusion among logged-in users. We refined this approach, retaining the disguise for reels, enhancing user comfort in public settings.

Wireframing & Prototyping:

Creating a light and inviting interface was vital to help users feel at ease. Safety was top priority, so we added an exit icon on each page for quick access to the ‘fake homepage,’ allowing users to exit quickly and discreetly.

Maintaining a sense of harmony to avoid overwhelming users with excessive information on each page was top priority. We stayed true to our chosen color schemes to ensure that interactive elements, such as buttons and icons, were readily identifiable to the user.

Conclusion:

Aimitis is a Progressive Web App (PWA) with a mission to empower women all across Ireland to education themselves on domestic violence, recognizing abuse and signs of toxic relationships. It offers a comprehensive resource center that breaks down how women can discreetly access help, whether through their PC, OBGYN, an A&E center or by accessing a women’s refugee center. Notably, Aimitis offers comprehensive list of every county in the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland. Most importantly, Aimitis offers a safe and secure platform for women to document their journey, upload images, and compile essential documentation if they need to bring legal action against their abuser for their own safety.
